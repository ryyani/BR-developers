TEST

Test cricket is the longest and oldest type of international cricket. The first officially
recognized Test match took place on 15-19 March 1877 and was played between England and Australia
at the Melbourne Cricket Ground, where Australia won by 45 runs.

ODI

One-Day International (ODI) cricket, a format renowned for its brevity and excitement, has etched a prominent place
in the world of cricket. Its origins can be traced back to the mid-20th century when cricketing nations sought to bring the
game closer to spectators by condensing it into a single day's play. The genesis of ODI cricket can be attributed to a need for innovation in the sport. The first official ODI match took place
on January 5, 1971, at the Melbourne Cricket Ground in Australia. It featured Australia and England, marking the inception
of a format that would capture the imagination of cricket fans worldwide. The impetus behind the creation of ODI cricket was a desire to provide a more spectator-friendly version of the game.
In traditional Test cricket, matches could span five days, making it inaccessible to a broader audience.
ODI cricket, with its concise format, allowed fans to witness an entire game in a single day.

T20

Twenty20 (T20) is a shortened format of cricket. At the professional level, it was introduced by the
England and Wales Cricket Board (ECB) in 2003 for the inter-county competition.In a Twenty20 game,
the two teams have a single innings each, which is restricted to a maximum of twenty overs.
Together with first-class and List A cricket, Twenty20 is one of the three forms of cricket recognised by the
International Cricket Council (ICC) as being played at the highest level, both internationally and domestically. A typical Twenty20 game is completed in about three and a half hours, with each innings lasting around 90 minutes and
an official 10-minute break between the innings. This is much shorter than previous forms of the game, and is closer to the timespan
of other popular team sports. It was introduced to create a fast-paced game that would be attractive to spectators at the ground
and viewers on television. The game has succeeded in spreading around the cricket world. On most international tours there is at least one Twenty20 match
and all Test-playing nations have a domestic cup competition.
